How do I add custom samples? Since LemmBeep doesn't have a web server of its own, you'll have to first upload your sample to a CORS (Cross-Origin Resource Sharing) -friendly website.
  • The easiest to use are File Garden or Catbox.
Next, you have to copy the URL of the sound itself. The URL should end with the filetype, such as .wav or .mp3.

To import it into LemmBeep, open the Edit tab, scroll to the bottom, and click on "Add Custom Samples..."

Paste your url and press "Okay." If everything goes smoothly, LemmBeep will reload and your sample will be available in the preset list.
Does LemmBeep have an offline version? Kind of- It does not have any executables or anything like UltraBox, but you can download everything here and it should work without an internet connection. Custom samples will not work sadly. I plan on getting this working when v1.0 comes out.
Does LemmBeep claim ownership over songs created with it? LemmBeep does not claim ownership over songs created with it, so original songs belong to their authors.

Neither LemmBeep or its creator assume responsibility for any copyrighted material played on LemmBeep. No songs are ever received, recorded, or distributed by LemmBeep's servers. All song data is contained in the URL after the hash (#) mark, and LemmBeep running inside your browser converts that data into sound waves.
